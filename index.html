Diplopia evaluation
Binocular diplopia workup
Binocular▾

Step 1 — Monocular vs binocular: Cover one eye. Diplopia resolves → binocular (ocular motor). Persists → monocular (optical: dry eye, irregular astigmatism, cataract, wrong Rx).

Step 2 — Characterize: Horizontal, vertical, or oblique? Constant or intermittent? Distance, near, or both? Gaze direction?

Step 3 — Cover test: Alternating cover test in all 9 positions of gaze. Note direction and magnitude.

Red flags → urgent referral:

  • New vertical diplopia (CN IV palsy, skew deviation)
  • Ptosis + diplopia (CN III palsy — rule out aneurysm)
  • Diplopia with headache, pain, or neurological signs
  • Pupil-involving CN III → urgent MRI/MRA same day
Maddox rod: red rod OS, white light at 6m. Horizontal diplopia → vertical prism; vertical diplopia → horizontal prism.
Headache evaluation
Visual & ocular causes
Neuro▾

Ocular causes to rule out:

  • Uncorrected refractive error — frontal/brow ache with near work; worse as day progresses
  • Convergence insufficiency — bifrontal HA with reading; NPC >10 cm
  • Acute angle closure — severe unilateral HA + red eye + halos + nausea; IOP often >40 mmHg
  • Giant cell arteritis (≥50 yrs) — temporal HA + scalp tenderness + jaw claudication + elevated ESR/CRP

Red flags:

  • Thunderclap onset ("worst headache of life")
  • New HA with fever, stiff neck, or rash
  • Positional HA worse lying down → elevated ICP
  • Visual field loss, APD, or papilledema
⚠ GCA is an emergency — same-day ESR/CRP + urgent rheumatology. Start steroids before biopsy if vision is threatened.

Stellest® lenses (Essilor)
Highest-efficacy spectacle lens for myopia control
H.A.L.T. Technology▾
Efficacy71% reduction in myopia progression vs. single-vision lenses (2-year clinical trial); clinically proven to reduce axial elongation by 53% over 2 years
TechnologyH.A.L.T. — 1,021 aspherical lenslets arranged in a constellation pattern, creating a volume of myopic defocus signal
Ideal candidateMyopic children 6–16 years; especially suited for ages 6–12 where atropine side effects are a concern or contact lenses are not appropriate; minimal adaptation period — most children are comfortable from day one
Wearing schedule≥10 hrs/day, 6+ days/week — higher efficacy with more hours worn
Rx rangeSphere –0.75 to –10.00 D; cylinder up to –4.00 D
AvailabilityLab-processed lens; available through major optical wholesale distributors
Stellest demonstrated 71% slowing of myopia progression in children wearing lenses ≥10 hrs/day, 6+ days/week — the highest published efficacy of any spectacle lens option. Ideal first choice for children ages 6–12 where atropine or contact lens use is not suitable.

Pharmacological▾
Dose0.01%–0.05% atropine, 1 drop QHS OU
Efficacy0.01%: ~50–60% slowing; 0.05%: ~60–70% slowing
ReboundMinimal at 0.01% vs. higher doses

Lifestyle & environmental factors
Adjunct recommendations for all patients
Lifestyle▾
  • Outdoor time: ≥90–120 min/day — strongest protective factor; retinal dopamine release
  • Near work breaks: 20-20-20 rule — every 20 min, look 20 ft away for 20 sec
  • Reading distance: Harmon distance (elbow to middle knuckle)
  • Axial length monitoring: Zeiss IOLMaster, Topcon Myah, or Heidelberg Myopia Watch every 6–12 months
